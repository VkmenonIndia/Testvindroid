c.Kurtosis:

  • Kurtosis measures the peakedness or flatness of a distribution compared to a normal distribution.
  • A kurtosis value of 0 indicates a normal distribution. Positive values indicate a relatively peaked distribution (leptokurtic), while negative values indicate a relatively flat distribution (platykurtic).
  1. Negative Kurtosis (Platykurtic):
  • A negative kurtosis value indicates that the distribution is flatter and has fewer outliers than a normal distribution.
  • In a platykurtic distribution, the tails of the distribution are lighter, meaning there are fewer extreme values compared to a normal distribution.
  • The central peak of the distribution is lower and broader than that of a normal distribution.
  • Negative kurtosis values are often associated with distributions that are more spread out and have lighter tails.
  • Age, MAle,Female bmi,Regions have less outliers
  1. Positive Kurtosis (Leptokurtic):
  • A positive kurtosis value indicates that the distribution is more peaked and has heavier tails than a normal distribution.
  • In a leptokurtic distribution, there are more extreme values, and the tails of the distribution are heavier compared to a normal distribution.
  • The central peak of the distribution is higher and narrower than that of a normal distribution.
  • Positive kurtosis values are often associated with distributions that are more concentrated around the mean and have heavier tails, indicating a higher probability of extreme values.
  • Smoker, Non smoker and charges have values more distributed around mean and have outliers since they are thick tailed

In summary, negative kurtosis suggests a flatter distribution with fewer outliers, while positive kurtosis suggests a more peaked distribution with more outliers.


d.Skewness is a measure of the asymmetry of the distribution of a dataset. It indicates whether the data are skewed to the left (negatively skewed), to the right (positively skewed), or are symmetrically distributed.

Here's what negative and positive skewness generally imply:

  1. Negative Skewness:
  • Negative skewness indicates that the distribution is skewed to the left.
  • In a negatively skewed distribution, the tail on the left side of the distribution is longer or fatter than the right side, and the majority of the data points are concentrated on the right side.
  • The mean is typically less than the median and mode in a negatively skewed distribution because the skewness is pulling the mean towards the lower tail.
  • Negative skewness is often associated with datasets where there are more extreme values on the left side, and the bulk of the values are concentrated on the right side.
  • MAle in slighly left sqewed and Non Smoker is Highly left skewed and majority of data in right side
  1. Positive Skewness:
  • Positive skewness indicates that the distribution is skewed to the right.
  • In a positively skewed distribution, the tail on the right side of the distribution is longer or fatter than the left side, and the majority of the data points are concentrated on the left side.
  • The mean is typically greater than the median and mode in a positively skewed distribution because the skewness is pulling the mean towards the higher tail.
  • Positive skewness is often associated with datasets where there are more extreme values on the right side, and the bulk of the values are concentrated on the left side.
  • All values except MAle and Non Smoker is Right skewed because data frequency in more concentrated on left side and highest being charges with more values skewed toards left side of SD

In summary, negative skewness suggests a distribution that is stretched towards the left, with a longer tail on the left side, while positive skewness suggests a distribution that is stretched towards the right, with a longer tail on the right side

F, P statistics

F-value of 500.811 typically indicates a strong statistical significance in the context of analysis of variance (ANOVA). In ANOVA, the F-statistic is calculated by comparing the variability between groups to the variability within groups.

Statistical Significance: A high F-value suggests that the variability between groups is significantly larger than the variability within groups. This indicates that there is likely a significant difference in the means of the groups being compared.

Probability (p-value): 1/1000 0r 0.001 is an extremely low p-value (close to 0). This indicates that the observed difference between groups is unlikely to be due to random chance alone and hence we can go ahead which ndicates strong evidence against the null hypothesis in hypothesis testing. 

ANOVA (Analysis of Variance) SUMMARY :

In summary, an F-value of 500.811 in ANOVA indicates strong evidence of a significant difference between groups being compared, with a very low probability that the observed difference is due to random chance.
